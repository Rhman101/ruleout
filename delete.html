Welcome to THE4124 Systematic Theology, a 12-credit course requiring an average of 120 hours of study, over an. 11-week period.

Systematic Theology is one of several subdisciplines offered in SATS’ Bachelor of Theology Honours program. This course offers a theoretical and biblical foundation to theology. However, without compromising academic quality, we hope that this course will also encourage you in your spiritual life and ministry because theology ought to ultimately aid us in our devotion to Christ Jesus.   

This course will begin by introducing you to Systematic Theology as a guide for Christian faith. You will be required to read J. I. Packer’s book, “Knowing God”, which will introduce you to the attributes of God, the theology of the Trinity, and the theology of the Word of God. In the next module, you will discover God’s relationship to creation and his providence. You will also study the spiritual realm—namely, angels and demons—and the physical realm, which includes humanity and ecology. Following this, you will learn about the person and work of Jesus Christ, salvation, and our future hope (eschatology). The Holy Spirit is the theme of the next module and here you will learn about his person and work and how he is actively involved in our personal lives and his Church. Lastly, you will discover the relationship between the kingdom of God and his Church.                                                     

THE4124 Systematic Theology combines extensive foundational reading with the opportunity to develop your research skills by completing a short research project, in addition to other learning activities.

By the end of this course the student should be able to:

  • Explain how Systematic Theology offers believers a guide for faith.
  • Describe the nature of God as revealed to us in Holy Scripture.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of God’s relationship to his creation.
  • Explain how God comes into our world to bring redemption.
  • Interpret the relationship between the kingdom of God and his Church.
  • Describe the person and work of the Holy Spirit among us.
